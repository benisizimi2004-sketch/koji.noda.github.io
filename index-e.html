ABOUT ME

I am conducting my graduation thesis project at Inoue Laboratory, Laboratory of Molecular Microbiology, Division of Applied Life Sciences, Graduate School of Agriculture, Kyoto University. The Inoue Laboratory utilizes genetic engineering with the model organism Saccharomyces cerevisiae to elucidate the cellular response mechanisms mediated by metabolites in the energy metabolism processes in a eukaryotic cell. My research topic, while slightly outside the main focus of the Inoue Lab, investigates the mechanism by which ε-polylysine, an oligopeptide antibiotics (used for food additives) discovered over 50 years ago, exhibits antimicrobial activity against fungi (such as molds). I am advancing this research not only through analyses using the genetic engineering and molecular biological experimental systems established in the Inoue Lab, but also by incorporating large-scale RNA-seq data analysis utilizing supercomputers managed and operated by the Kyoto University Institute for Chemical Research.
