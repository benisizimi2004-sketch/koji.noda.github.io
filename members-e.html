BIOGRAPHY

INTRODUCTION

　I’m now an undergraduate student (B4) striving daily to earn my PhD in the field of life science. In my undergraduate education, I majored biochemistry, molecular biology, and cell biology, but I have also learned probability and statistics, machine learning, bioinformatics, and mathematical biology. While Python is my primary research language, I frequently code R , espeically for statistics analysis. Captivated by the fascination of research during high school, I am currently studying the mechanism of action of antifungal agents used as food additives. Leveraging my self-taught coding skills, I perform large-scale data processing for RNA-seq analysis on supercomputers. Through comprehensive gene expression analysis, GO analysis, and network analysis, I aim to elucidate a unique mechanism of action model for antifungal agents and discover target proteins.
In graduate school, I plan to study structural epigenetics using single-molecule nucleosome imaging.
